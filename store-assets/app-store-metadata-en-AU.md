### Keywords
> 100-character limit, comma-separated, no spaces after commas. Do not repeat words already in the app name or subtitle.

**`mental health,journalling,teens,youth,mindfulness,gratitude,grounding,anxiety,wellbeing,affirmations`**
_100 / 100 characters_

**Rationale (AU-specific):**
- `mental health` — primary search intent; high-volume in AU (influenced by campaigns from Beyond Blue, headspace, RUOK Day)
- `journalling` — AU/UK double-l spelling matches how Australian users type the term; core feature
- `teens` / `youth` — audience qualifiers
- `mindfulness` — overlaps with breathing and grounding; well-recognised in AU school wellbeing programmes
- `gratitude` — core ritual step, searched as a standalone term
- `grounding` — 5-4-3-2-1 technique name; searched by name
- `anxiety` — high-volume AU search term; the problem this app addresses
- `wellbeing` — strongly preferred over "wellness" in Australian English and AU health/education sector language
- `affirmations` — replaces "ritual" (base locale) with a higher-volume AU search term; matches the "I am" affirmation feature directly; popular in AU youth mental health and school wellbeing contexts

## Localisation notes

### Spelling changes from base (en) locale

| Base spelling | en-AU spelling | Field(s) affected |
|---|---|---|
| `journaling` | `journalling` | Keywords, short description, full description |
| `practice` (noun used as verb) | `practise` (verb) | Description ("three-part practise", "practises are short") |
| `practices` | `practises` | Description |

### Tone changes from base locale

- **"young people"** → **"young Australians"** in the "Built for Young People" section of both the App Store and Play Store descriptions. This grounds the product in the local market and is a genuine differentiator for Australian App Store / Play Store ranking on region-specific queries.
- Tone is otherwise unchanged — the base copy is already warm and direct, which aligns well with the Australian register.

### Keyword strategy changes (App Store only)

- Swapped `ritual` → `affirmations`: "affirmations" has higher search volume in the AU App Store for this audience segment, directly maps to the "I am" feature, and is well-established in AU school wellbeing programme language.
- `journalling` (double-l) replaces `journaling` to match how AU users spell the term in search.
- `wellbeing` is retained (over "wellness") — the dominant term in Australian health, education, and government wellbeing contexts.
